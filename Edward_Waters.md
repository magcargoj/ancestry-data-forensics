## Biography

Edward Waters is one of the most storied figures in early colonial Virginia, known for his multiple survivals against extraordinary odds.

### 🚢 The Sea Venture Shipwreck (1609)
In 1609, Edward was a passenger on the ***Sea Venture***, the flagship of the "Third Supply" intended for Jamestown. The ship was caught in a massive hurricane and deliberately run aground on the reefs of **Bermuda**. Edward survived the wreck and spent ten months on the island while the survivors built new ships. During this time, he was involved in an altercation where he killed another sailor in self-defense and lived as a fugitive in the Bermuda woods to escape execution.

### 🏝️ The "Three Kings of Bermuda"
When the rest of the survivors finally sailed for Jamestown in 1610, Edward remained behind with two others. For two years, these three men were the only human inhabitants of Bermuda, earning them the nickname the **"Three Kings of Bermuda."** During this time, they discovered a fortune in **ambergris** (a rare substance used in perfumes).

### ⚔️ Capture and Escape (1622)
After eventually reaching Virginia, Edward became a prominent settler and married [[Grace_Neale]]. During the Great Indian Massacre of 1622, Edward and Grace were captured by the Nansemond tribe. They managed a daring escape by boat across the river to reach the safety of the English settlements.

Edward later served as a **Burgess** and was designated an "Ancient Planter," a title given to those who arrived in Virginia before 1616 and survived the early hardships of the colony.
