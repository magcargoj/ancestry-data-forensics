## Biography

William Leete was a foundational figure in early Connecticut. He emigrated from England in 1639 and was one of the founders of Guilford, CT. He served as Governor of the New Haven Colony (1661–1665) and later as Governor of the Connecticut Colony (1676–1683). He is famously known for protecting the regicides (judges who signed the death warrant of King Charles I) from the agents of King Charles II.
