# Service & Sacrifice: Military History

From the struggle for American independence to the industrial era, our family has a long and storied history of military service.

## 🇺🇸 The Revolutionary War
Several ancestors fought for the birth of the United States, primarily in the North Carolina regiments.
*   **[[William_Wood_1758]] (1758–1844):** A documented veteran of the Revolutionary War who served in the North Carolina line.
*   **[[Hardin_Warner_Sr]] (1755–1829):** Served in the 5th North Carolina Regiment, enlisting in 1779.
*   **[[Willis_AKA_Wills_Wilson_Sr]] (1752–1833):** A private in the North Carolina line and a Pitt County pioneer.

## ⚔️ The Civil War
The 1860s saw our family members serving in critical roles, often at a high personal cost.
*   **[[John_Charles_Teal]] (1838–1918):** A Private in the "**Pee Dee Wild Cats**" (Co. K, 26th NC Infantry). He was **wounded at the Battle of Gettysburg** on July 1, 1863, during his regiment's famous assault on McPherson’s Ridge.
*   **[[Calvin_D_Brooks]] (1832–1862):** Enlisted in the "**Ashe Beaver Creek Guards**" (Co. A, 37th NC Infantry). He made the ultimate sacrifice, **dying in a Knoxville hospital** in 1862 during the unit's deployment to Tennessee.
*   **[[William_B_Joe_Moye]] (1825–1865):** Served in the Confederate army and died in Louisiana in the final months of the war.

## 🛡️ Colonial Militia
In the early 1600s, family members were active in the protection of the early colonies.
*   **[[Edward_Waters]] (1584–1630):** An "Ancient Planter" who survived the 1622 massacre and served as a colonial leader.
*   **[[William_Waters_Sr]] (1623–1689):** Held the rank of **Lieutenant Colonel** in the Virginia Militia and served as High Sheriff.
