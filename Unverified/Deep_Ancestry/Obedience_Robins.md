## Biography

Colonel Obedience Robins was a surgeon and a prominent political figure on the Eastern Shore of Virginia. He was instrumental in naming Northampton County after his home shire in England. He served as a member of the House of Burgesses and the Governor's Council.

After the death of his first wife, Margaret, he married [[Grace_Neale]], the widow of Edward Waters. This marriage brought together the Robins and Waters families, leading to the later marriage of his daughter [[Margaret_Robins_Clark]] to his stepson [[William_Waters_Sr]].
