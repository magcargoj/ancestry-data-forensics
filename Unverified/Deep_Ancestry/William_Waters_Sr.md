## Biography

Colonel William Waters Sr. was a prominent figure in 17th-century colonial Virginia. He served as a member of the House of Burgesses for Northampton County and held the rank of Lieutenant Colonel in the local militia. He also served as the High Sheriff of Northampton County.

His marriage to [[Margaret_Robins_Clark]] around 1650 is a point of genealogical interest. They were step-siblings; William's mother, Grace Neale, married Margaret's father, Obedience Robins, in 1634 after the death of William's father. While some traditions claim they were first cousins, there is no primary evidence for a blood relationship.

His will, proved in 1689, lists six sons but does not mention a son named Morgan, casting doubt on traditional lineages that link him to the later Georgia Waters family via a "Morgan William Waters."
