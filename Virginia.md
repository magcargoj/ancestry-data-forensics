# Colonial Virginia Pioneers

Our family was among the earliest English settlers in North America, establishing roots on the **Eastern Shore** and the **Tidewater** regions of Virginia in the early 1600s.

## 🏛️ Political & Social Leadership
The **Waters** and **Robins** families were prominent in the political life of early Virginia. These ancestors served as surgeons, High Sheriffs, and members of the **House of Burgesses**, helping to shape the laws and structure of the burgeoning colony.

## 🚢 The Sea Venture Connection
Our ancestor **[[Edward_Waters]]** arrived in Virginia under extraordinary circumstances. He was a survivor of the ***Sea Venture*** shipwreck in Bermuda in 1609, an event famously believed to have inspired Shakespeare's *The Tempest*. After living as one of the "Three Kings of Bermuda" for two years, he finally reached Virginia and became a founding member of the colony.

## 🌳 Notable Ancestors
*   **[[William_Waters_Sr]]** (1623–1689): A Burgess and High Sheriff of Northampton County.
*   **[[Obedience_Robins]]** (1601–1662): A prominent surgeon and political leader who named Northampton County after his home in England.
*   **[[Grace_Neale]]** (1603–1682): A resilient matriarch who survived the early hardships of the colony and merged two influential lineages.
